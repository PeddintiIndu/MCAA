About AFM

Accounting and financial management are distinct but complementary disciplines crucial for any business. 
Accounting focuses on recording, summarizing, and reporting financial transactions, primarily for external stakeholders
 and internal decision-making. Financial management, on the other hand, is concerned with planning, organizing, directing,
 and controlling the financial resources of an organization to achieve its goals.
